Thanks for the OTA 22bf rundown — confirmed the false-block fix on our side too.

1. Multi-queue job-type strings

Dispatch normalises every job to a single lowercase serviceType field on the job record. The values you'll see are:

serviceType	Meaning	Queue cap
taxi	Standard taxi ride (default if missing)	1 queued
tm	Total Mobility	1 queued
food	Food / restaurant delivery	multiple OK
freight	Parcel / freight delivery	multiple OK
Notes:

The field is serviceType (camelCase). We also tolerate ServiceType as an alias on inbound payloads, but every job we emit uses lowercase serviceType.
Inbound aliases we normalise: restaurant → food, delivery → freight. You only need to gate on the four normalised strings above.
Rentals and towing are not first-class serviceType values yet — they currently fall under taxi. Treat them as multi-queue once we add the strings; I'll ping you when we do.
Anything missing or unknown should default to taxi (1 queued) — same fail-safe our server uses.
Server-side cap already shipped to match this policy: taxi and tm keep the 1-queued cap, food and freight are uncapped. Server is ready when the driver app catches up.

2. D002's OTA version for trips 6112605171 / 6112605173

Those four trips have no AppVersion / runtime / OTA-group stamped on the closed-job record. They completed before we wired runtime capture into the trip-summary handler, so we genuinely can't tell you which OTA D002 was on at that moment.

Next time you need to ask "which OTA was D002 on for trip #X" we can just look it up.
