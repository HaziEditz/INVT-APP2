What Total Mobility (TM) Actually Is
Total Mobility is a New Zealand government subsidy scheme. Eligible passengers (usually disabled or mobility-impaired) get a TM card issued by their local council. When they take a taxi, they only pay a subsidised portion of the fare (typically 50%, up to a council-set cap). The taxi company then claims the remaining portion back from the council.

The money flow:

Passenger pays their half in the taxi
Driver records the trip with the TM card number
Company submits a monthly claim to the council
Council reimburses the company for all the subsidised trips that month
How the Admin Side Currently Works
The super admin reads trip data from Firebase. The key paths are:

trips/{cid}/{tripId}/ — individual trip records written by the driver app
Each trip needs: card number, passenger name, fare total, passenger amount, TM subsidy amount, date/time, driver ID
The admin pages built so far:

TM-Cards.aspx — manages which TM cards are registered, active, or suspended per council
TM-Trips.aspx — views all trips by company/date, flags unusual ones
TM-Flagged.aspx — trips automatically or manually flagged for review (over-cap, repeated within a timeframe, etc.)
TM-Reports.aspx — generates the monthly claim report per council to submit for reimbursement
TM-Council-Config.aspx — sets the subsidy rate, cap per trip, council name and contact details
Council Portal — the council logs in to view their monthly reports and download claim summaries
What the Driver App Currently Does for TM
Based on what the admin reads, the driver app currently:

Has a "Total Mobility" payment type option
Accepts a TM card number (manual entry or card reader)
Writes the completed trip to trips/{cid}/{tripId}/ in Firebase with the card number and amounts
That data flows straight to the admin with no validation happening first
What the Driver App Should Do (But Doesn't Yet)
Card Validation at the time of booking
Right now the driver types a card number and it just gets recorded. The app should check Firebase before accepting — is this card registered? Is it active? Has it been suspended? If the card isn't in tmCards/{cid}/{cardNum}/ or is marked inactive, the driver should see a warning before processing the trip.

Council cap enforcement
Each council sets a maximum TM subsidy per trip (e.g., the passenger pays 50% up to a $35 cap). The driver app should calculate this automatically based on the TM-Council-Config settings — show the driver: "Passenger owes $12.50, TM covers $12.50." Right now this calculation likely happens manually or not at all in the app.

Duplicate/suspicious trip detection
The app should warn the driver if the same card has been used very recently (within the same hour, for example), as this is a common fraud pattern. Currently the admin flags these after the fact in TM-Flagged.aspx, but it would be better to catch it at the time of booking.

Trip category / purpose
Some councils want to know the trip category (medical, social, employment). The driver app should have a dropdown for this, and it gets recorded with the trip. Currently TM-Trips.aspx shows trips but there's no category data coming from the app.

Receipt / confirmation
When the trip is complete, the driver app could show a confirmation screen: "TM trip recorded — Passenger paid $X, TM covers $Y" — giving the driver something to show the passenger if needed.

Summary of the Gap
The admin side is fully built and ready to receive data. The driver app currently sends basic trip data (card number + amounts), which is the minimum needed to generate council reports.

The missing pieces on the driver app are: real-time card validation, automatic cap calculation, trip category, and suspicious trip warnings. None of these block operations right now — they just make it more accurate and reduce manual follow-up work in the flagged trips queue.
