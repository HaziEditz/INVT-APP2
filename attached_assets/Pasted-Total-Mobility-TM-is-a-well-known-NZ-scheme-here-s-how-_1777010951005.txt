Total Mobility (TM) is a well-known NZ scheme — here's how it actually works and how it could fit into the app:

How Total Mobility works in NZ

Eligible passengers (elderly, disabled) carry a physical TM card issued by their local council
The taxi operator is a registered TM provider
The council subsidises 50% of the metered fare, capped at a fixed dollar amount (the cap varies by council — e.g. $37 in some regions means the max subsidy is $18.50, so council covers up to $18.50 and the passenger pays the rest)
The taxi company claims the subsidy from the council — the passenger only pays their half at the time of the ride
Traditionally this is done with paper vouchers or swipe card terminals
What needs to happen in the app

There are two sides:

Driver side (your app):

Before or at trip completion, driver marks the job as "Total Mobility" and enters the passenger's TM card number
App calculates the split — e.g. full fare $30 → subsidy $15, passenger pays $15. Or full fare $80 → subsidy capped at $18.50, passenger pays $61.50
Driver collects the passenger's portion (cash or card)
The job is recorded with subsidy amount flagged for council claim
Back-end / reporting side:

The company needs a record of all TM trips with: card number, full fare, subsidy amount, passenger amount, date/driver
These get batched and submitted to council for reimbursement (this is typically done by admin, not the driver)
Things you need to decide before building:

Card validation — do you want to validate TM card numbers against a list, or just let the driver type whatever number the passenger shows?
Payment collection — is the passenger's portion always cash, or can they pay by card through the app?
Which councils? — different councils have different caps. Do you need to handle multiple caps or just one?
Voucher/receipt — does the passenger need a printed or emailed receipt showing the subsidy split?
Admin portal — does your dispatcher console need a TM claims report, or is that handled separately?
Passenger app — you mentioned the passenger app too. Would a passenger be able to flag themselves as TM when booking, or only the driver handles it at ride time?
